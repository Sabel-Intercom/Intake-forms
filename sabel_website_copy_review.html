Beat · Discovery checklist

Discovery checklist

Before any tool gets configured, four questions decide whether AI works for your team or just adds noise. Walk through them with us in discovery and you'll have a clearer view of what to automate, what to leave alone, and what to fix first.

✓

What AI should achieve

Pin down the outcomes that actually matter: deflection rate, time to resolution, CX score, or freeing humans for the conversations that need a human. Without that target, AI becomes a feature you bought instead of a result you measure.

✓

Current customer experience reality

Map what customers actually experience today, not what your process diagram says they should. Where do they wait, repeat themselves, or churn? That baseline is what every future automation gets measured against.

✓

Planned AI use

Decide where AI is going to operate: front-line resolution, agent assist, internal knowledge lookup, or all three. Each path has different readiness needs, different risks, and a different speed to value.

✓

Where AI should and should not be used

Some conversations belong with a human, full stop. Billing disputes, vulnerable customers, complex retention. Drawing those lines early protects your brand and gives Fin a cleaner scope to be excellent at.

Beat · Blueprint deliverables

Blueprint deliverables

Six artefacts that turn the discovery into something your team can actually run with. Each one is a working document, not a slide deck. You'll keep using them long after we've handed over.

Roles

A clear picture of who does what in an AI-forward support function: human support, AI operations, knowledge owners, and the optimisation function that keeps automation improving.

Governance

The decision rights, approval gates, and review rhythms that keep AI safe at scale. Who can change Guidance, who signs off on a new Procedure, and how changes get tested before they go live.

Knowledge Strategy

A plan for the content Fin actually needs: what to write, what to retire, how to keep it accurate, and how to structure articles so AI can resolve from them rather than just quote them.

Workflows

The end-to-end paths customers travel, mapped against where Fin, Workflows, agents, and Procedures each carry the load. This is where the operating model becomes operational.

KPIs

The metrics that actually prove transformation is working: automation rate, resolution rate, CX score, and the leading indicators that tell you when something's drifting before customers notice.

Ownership

Named owners for every part of the system. Knowledge, Guidance, Procedures, integrations, monitors. No orphaned automation, no "I thought Sarah had that."

Beat · How AI Automation increases (intro rail)

How AI Automation increases

Lifting automation rate isn't one big change, it's three small levers pulled in the right order. Set the attributes that let Fin understand context, give Fin the guidance it needs to behave like your best agent, and tune escalation so handoffs happen at the right moment, not the first sign of friction. Get those three right and the curve takes care of itself.

Attributes
The data Fin reads to understand who's asking and why. Plan, region, tenure, account status, custom attributes from your systems. The more Fin knows about the customer before it answers, the more relevant the answer.
Guidance
How Fin behaves in plain English. Tone, terminology, things to always do, things to never promise, behaviour rules that apply across every conversation, and topic-specific instructions when the standard answer needs nuance.
Escalation
When to hand off and where to send the conversation. Some escalations are rule-based on attributes (VIP tier, region, ticket type). Others are guidance-driven on context (someone mentions injury, a health risk, or a legal threat — escalate immediately).
Beat · Guidance

Tell Fin how to sound, what to say, and what to stay clear of

Guidance is where Fin learns to behave like your best agent on their best day. Tone of voice, preferred terminology, things to never promise, exceptions to the standard answer. Set it once and every Fin reply lines up with the brand, the legal position, and the customer experience you've worked hard to build everywhere else.

Beat · Quality & guardrails (4 blocks)

Quality & guardrails

Automation only scales if you trust it. We set up the monitors, thresholds, and review rhythms that catch drift early and keep Fin operating inside the lines you set.

Monitors & Scorecards

Continuously evaluate Fin's conversation quality at scale. Monitors define which conversations get reviewed, from random samples to high-risk signals like low CX scores or policy breaches. Custom Scorecards define how each one is judged. No more spreadsheet QA, no more sampling by gut feel.

Human-in-the-loop approvals

For high-risk actions, Fin pauses inside a Procedure and waits for a teammate to approve before continuing. Refunds, account closures, goodwill gestures, anything policy says needs a human sign-off. Fin keeps control of the conversation, your team keeps control of the decisions that matter.

Sample reviews

Weekly human review of a structured sample of Fin's resolutions. Not every conversation, just enough to catch drift early and feed the next training round.

Pass rate tracking

Every monitor reports a pass rate. When it drops, you know exactly where to look, what to fix, and whether the fix worked.

Beat · Connect
CONNECT

Connect to the source, read-only, on day one

Migr8Now connects to your existing platform, Zendesk, Freshdesk, Zoho, HelpScout, LiveChat, or a bespoke source, using read-only credentials. Nothing moves, nothing changes. We're just looking at what you have so we can size the work accurately and show you exactly what's coming across.

Beat · Sample
SAMPLE

Sample a slice before committing to the full run

Before a single ticket migrates for real, we pull a representative sample into a sandbox Intercom workspace. You see your conversations rendered in their new home, with attachments, attributes, and tags intact, and we resolve any mapping questions while the stakes are low.

Beat · Validate
VALIDATE

Validate the mapping before anything goes live

Tickets, users, companies, tags, custom attributes, and conversation history all need to map cleanly from the source to Intercom's data model. We walk you through the mapping document, get sign-off on every field, and run validation queries so nothing gets lost or silently reshaped in transit.

Beat · Closed Run
CLOSED RUN

Run the full migration with the source still live

The bulk of your history moves in a closed run while your team keeps working in the source platform. No downtime, no frozen inbox, no agents waiting around. By the time we're ready to cut over, the vast majority of your data is already in Intercom and verified.

Beat · Delta
DELTA

Catch the new closed tickets, and bring the live ones across exactly as they are

The delta run does two jobs. First, it picks up any tickets that closed in the source platform while the closed run was completing, so the historical record stays complete. Second, every open, pending, and waiting-on-customer ticket moves into Intercom in exactly the state it was in, with the full conversation thread, attachments, and context intact. Your agents pick them up in Intercom from precisely where they left off in the source platform. No re-opening, no re-explaining to the customer, no work in flight that gets dropped.

Beat · Cutover
CUTOVER

Switch over with the team trained and the day-one plan ready

Cutover is the moment the source goes read-only and Intercom becomes the system of record. We sequence it for a low-volume window, with the team trained, the day-one operational plan agreed, and a rollback path documented but rarely needed. Most teams are answering tickets in Intercom within the hour.

Beat · Data integrity and mapping

Data integrity and mapping

Every record that moves gets validated against what was in the source. Tickets match, users match, attachments load, custom attributes carry across, historical conversations stay searchable. The integrity report is yours to keep. If anything ever looks off six months from now, the evidence of what came over and how is in writing.
